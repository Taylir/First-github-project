  • (p): This is used for general writing of whatever really. All of these notes are put into a p label as they are long and not used as a heading so they will fall under the p label and will mainly just be paragraph.

  • (section): Section is used to seperate well sections. If you have one area talking about one thing or a topic or a list. You would use a section label when wanting to start a new one and to seperate it from the last.

  • (div): Div is used to help seperate other lables that do not automatically seperate as well as being able to label them easier since you sometimes want certain CSS effects on other pieces but not all of them.

  • (ID & Class): These are used withing each of the other labels to help create certain classes so that you can design and decerate certain parts of the webpage to make it look better. Basically creating a call to those certain areas for CSS.

  • (ul & ol): These labels are meant to make un-organized lists and organized lists (ul & ol respectivley) so that you can make things in a more listed format. The main difference is that Ul is more of just a random listing in any order while an ol is a very organized top to bottom ordered list.

    • (li): For these lists items you want to seperate them with li creating each item in said list as a listed item.

  • (Header & footer): Header is used typically first or right after the Nav depending on your page layout but you want this to be at the top as header kind of suggests. And oppisite with footer as you want this to be just about very last outside of body unlike any other label but just before the HTML label at the end, Footer will typically be all by istelf at the very end.

  • (a): a is used as an anchor for links, typically followed by the keyword href, will allow links to be ancorhed in and allowing the work form or whatever you may chose to be clicked and taken to said link.

  • (button): A very simple to understand but the practicle use of it it is more difficult. Creats a button on a page but in most cases will be used by putting button in a form in order to actually allow it to take you to a link.

  • (form): Form is a very powerful and very used label in almost every webpage. It may be more hidden or you may not realize what is actually using the form tag but it helps creat a section for making a form and allowing it enter credetials for emails and names and anything else really.(Css added to the bottom form to make it a bit more organized and less ugly, the first form has no css added to it.     • (label): In forms this is used to label the following input such as username: or like Email:.
